Gorana Dance

Program Line Up

All dancers are listed in order of appearance in italics.

1. Самодива (Samodiva)

A samodiva is a woodland fairy or nymph, with spiritual energy drawn from nature.

Nadia (Solo)

2. Комитско Либе (Komitsko Libe)

A dance of commitment from the Pirin region in southwest Bulgaria.

Venci, Galina, Elena, Emilia, Inna, Chavdar

3. Пирински (Pirinski)

A female ritual dance from the Pirin region in southwest Bulgaria.

White costumes: Venci, Galina, Elena, Emilia, Inna, Mihaela
Red costumes: Guergana, Tina, Iva, Jianying, Maria, Nicole

4. Гинка (Ginka)

Ginka is a female name. This dance is common in Bulgaria and originates from the Pirin region.

Evgeni, Tina, Jianying, Iva, Guergana, Maria, Nicole, Chavdar

5. Джангурица (Dzanguritsa)

A common dance from the Pirin region in southwest Bulgaria.

Stage left: Inna, Elena, Venci, Tina
Stage right: Galina, Emilia, Mihaela, Guergana

6. Songs from Vlada Tomova (Yasna Voices)

  • Брала мома (A girl picking flowers)
  • Кузум Еленке (Beautiful Elenke)
  • Тръгнала e малка мома (A little girl left)

7. Буенек (Buenek)

A female dance connected with spring rituals and traditionally danced on Еньовден (Enyov Day / St. John's Eve). The wreaths are made from flowers and herbs believed to have a healing power.

Stage left: Galina, Jianying, Nicole, Emilia, Maria, Iva
Stage right: Inna, Elena, Venci, Tina, Mihaela, Guergana

8. Song from Lubomir Smilenov

  • Сворнати хора (Svornati hora)

9. Копаница (Kopanitsa)*

A dance from western Bulgaria.

*Performed by Algara Dance:
Galina S., Albena, Bisera, Galina N., Iskra, Ioana, Kalina, Krasimira, Maria R., Maria B., Spassena, Tanya, Toska, Vessela P., Vessela D., Virginiya

10. Songs from Vlada Tomova (Yasna Voices)

  • Снощи си дойде (Last night he came back)
  • Бачо Никола (Brother Nikola)

11. Шира (Shira)

A dance from Bulgaria's Danube (northern) region, referencing the Danube river that runs along the border with Romania.

Evgeni, Nicole, Tina, Guergana, Emilia, Mihaela, ...

12. Северняшки Сюита (Severniashki Suite)

A dance from Bulgaria's northern region ("severniashki" translates to "northern").

Stage left: Chavdar, Inna, Emilia, Tina, Mihaela
Stage right: Venci, Galina, Elena, Guergana

13. Songs from Petya Maglova

  • Мъри Маро (Muri Maro)
  • А бре юначе (Oh young man)

14. Songs from Hristo Shehov

  • Руфинка болна легнала (Rufinka went to bed sick)
  • Айде Калино да идем (Come on Kalino, let's go)

15. Еркечки (Erkechki)

A ritual Easter dance originating from Kozichino, a village in eastern Bulgaria close to the Black Sea. The village is also known as Erkech, a portmanteau from the Turkish "erken geç" (go faster) dating back to the Ottoman Empire.

This song is from the Philip Kutev National Folklore Ensemble and belongs to The Golden Collection of Bulgarian Folk Heritage.

Stage right: ...

  • Шинко ле (Shinko le)

17. Варненски (Varnenski)

A dance from Varna, a city on the Black Sea.

Stage left: Inna, Guergana, Mihaela, Jianying, Chavdar, Evgeni
Stage right: Emilia, Tina, Maria, Nicole

18. Song from Hristo

  • Соньо, мой соньо (Sonyo, my sonyo)

19. Криво Садовско (Krivo Sadovsko)

A dance from Sadovo, a village in midsouthern Bulgaria close to Plovdiv. "Krivo" translates to "crooked".

Chavdar, Venci, Elena, Tina, Mihaela, Emilia, Galina, Inna
